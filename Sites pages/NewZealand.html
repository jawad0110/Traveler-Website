The total cost of the flights will vary depending on the time of year and the airline you choose, but you can expect to pay around USD 1,200-1,500. There are several ways to get around New Zealand once you arrive, including buses, trains, and rental cars. The cost of transportation will vary depending on the distance you are traveling and the mode of transportation you choose. For example, a bus pass for unlimited travel for one week costs around USD 100. Overall, the cost of traveling to New Zealand from Macau without returning will vary depending on your travel preferences and budget, but you can expect to spend around USD 1,300-1,800.

The best time to visit New Zealand depends on your interests and the activities you wish to pursue. For those seeking sunshine and outdoor activities, the summer months (December to February) offer warm temperatures and long daylight hours, ideal for hiking, swimming, and sightseeing. However, this peak season also brings increased crowds and higher prices. For a quieter and more affordable experience, consider visiting during the months (March-April and September-October) when the weather is generally pleasant, and crowds are smaller. Winter (June-August) offers unique opportunities for snow sports and skiing in the Southern Alps, but some attractions may be closed or have reduced hours. Spring (November) bursts with blooming flowers, creating a picturesque landscape. Ultimately, the best time to visit New Zealand is the season that aligns with your travel preferences, whether it be soaking up the sun, embracing adventure, or enjoying the serenity of nature.
